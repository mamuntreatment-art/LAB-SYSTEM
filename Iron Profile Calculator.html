An Iron Profile (also known as an Iron Panel or Iron Status Test) is a group of blood tests used to evaluate how much iron is in your body and how well it is being transported.

Here is the standard breakdown of the test names included in a complete Iron Profile:

Standard Iron Profile Components

Serum Iron: Measures the actual amount of iron circulating in your blood liquid (serum).

Serum Ferritin: Measures the amount of stored iron in your body. This is often the most critical marker for detecting early iron deficiency.

Total Iron-Binding Capacity (TIBC): Measures how well proteins (specifically transferrin) can bind and carry iron through your blood.

Transferrin Saturation (TSAT): This is a calculated percentage ((Serum Iron ÷ TIBC) × 100) that shows how many of your iron-binding sites are actually carrying iron.

Additional Related Tests (Often ordered together)

Depending on the lab or your doctor's requirements, an Iron Profile is frequently paired with:

CBC (Complete Blood Count): Specifically looking at Hemoglobin (Hb), Packed Cell Volume (PCV/Hematocrit), and MCV/MCH to see if the iron levels have started affecting your red blood cells (causing anemia).

Transferrin: The actual measurement of the protein that binds iron (sometimes ordered instead of or alongside TIBC).

Tip for Patients: For the most accurate results, an Iron Profile usually requires 8 to 12 hours of fasting (no food or drink except water) before the blood draw, and it is ideally done in the morning.
